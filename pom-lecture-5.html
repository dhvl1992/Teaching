Scientific Management

Frederick Winslow Taylor (1856-1915)

1. Elements of Scientific Management

🤔 What is Scientific Management?

Simple Definition: Scientific Management is like using a GPS for work - it finds the most efficient route to complete any task by studying it scientifically, rather than relying on guesswork.

Taylor's Problem & Solution:

In the 1880s, workers basically guessed how to do their jobs. Taylor believed there was a scientifically best way to do every job - the "One Best Way".

Memory Trick: "SSTMDM" = Smart Students Think More, Do More

1. 🔬 Scientific Determination of Task (S)

Concept: Break big jobs into small pieces, study each piece scientifically.

ISRO Example: Mars mission broken into thousands of specific tasks - fuel calculations, navigation, communication. Each studied scientifically. Cost: only ₹450 crores!

2. 👥 Scientific Selection (S)

Concept: Choose people based on their abilities, not politics or favoritism.

Indian Railways: Train drivers selected through fitness tests, psychological assessments, technical exams.

3. 🎓 Scientific Training (T)

Concept: Teach people systematically using proven methods.

Infosys: 3-month systematic training at Mysore campus for all new engineers.

4. ⏱️ Time & Motion Study (M)

Concept: Study movements and timing, eliminate waste.

Dabbawalas: Every movement timed and optimized for 99.999% accuracy.

5. 💰 Differential Payment (D)

Concept: Pay based on performance - more output, more money.

Flipkart: Warehouse bonuses during sales based on targets met.

6. 🤝 Mental Revolution (M)

Concept: Change from "us vs them" to cooperation.

Tata Group: Management and employees worked together during 26/11 attacks.

🎯 Key Takeaways - Elements of Scientific Management:

  • Scientific method replaces guesswork in work design
  • Each element focuses on finding the "one best way"
  • Success requires both management commitment and worker cooperation
  • Modern applications: Manufacturing, IT, services, project management

2. Time and Motion Study

TWO SIMPLE QUESTIONS:

⏰ How long should this take?
🎯 What movements are necessary?

Eliminate waste, optimize flow

Simple Definition:

Time Study: Finding the standard time needed for a task (like timing how long different math problems take).

Motion Study: Eliminating unnecessary movements (like organizing your desk so everything is within reach).

🍕 Domino's 30-Minute Example:

  • Kitchen layout: Designed for minimum walking
  • Ingredients: Placed within arm's reach
  • Assembly line: Each person does specific task
  • Delivery routes: GPS-optimized paths

🏭 Maruti Suzuki Application:

Assembly line workers' every movement studied and optimized. Result: Can assemble a car in specific time with consistent quality.

🎯 Key Takeaways - Time and Motion Study:

  • Time study measures "how long," motion study improves "how"
  • Focus on eliminating waste, not making people work harder
  • Modern applications: App design, surgery, manufacturing
  • Requires worker cooperation for success

3. Differential Payment System and Scientific Training

💰 Differential Payment System

Simple Definition:

Differential Payment is like cricket scoring - you get rewarded based on your performance. Hit more runs (produce more), get higher rewards.

How It Works:

  • Standard Output: Minimum expected (like par in golf)
  • Below Standard: Lower pay rate
  • Above Standard: Higher pay rate
  • No middle ground: Either low rate or high rate

Modern Indian Example - Zomato Delivery:

  • Base rate: ₹30 per delivery
  • Peak hours: ₹50 per delivery
  • Distance bonus: ₹10 extra for >5km
  • Rating bonus: 4.5+ stars = 20% extra
Benefits: Motivates performance, rewards efficiency. Challenges: May create unhealthy competition, stress.

🎓 Scientific Training

Simple Definition:

Scientific Training means teaching the exact skills needed using proven methods, not hoping people figure it out themselves.

4-Step Method:

  1. Show them the right way
  2. Explain why it's done this way
  3. Let them try while you watch
  4. Check their performance regularly

Memory: SELC = Smart Engineering Leads to Competence

TCS Training Example:

  • 3-month program: Theory + hands-on practice
  • Standardized curriculum: Same training everywhere
  • Regular assessments: Pass/fail based on performance
  • Continuous improvement: Training updated with new techniques

Result: Consistent quality across 400,000+ employees globally.

4. Followers of Taylor

Simple Definition:

Followers of Taylor were management thinkers who built upon Taylor's ideas, adding their own innovations and improvements to scientific management.

Henry L. Gantt (1861-1919)

Main Innovation: Gantt Charts

Visual timeline showing what tasks need to be done, when they start/end, and which tasks depend on others.

Delhi Metro Example: Used Gantt charts to manage complex construction - land acquisition, design, foundation, pillars, tracks, testing. Each phase planned and tracked visually.
Modern Use: Project management software, construction, IT projects, event planning.

Frank & Lillian Gilbreth

Innovation: Therbligs (Motion Elements)

Identified 18 basic hand movements that make up any job - reach, grasp, move, position, use, release.

Apollo Hospitals: Surgery procedures optimized using motion study - instruments arranged in standard order, minimal hand movements during operations.
Bricklaying Innovation: Reduced motions from 18 to 5, tripled productivity.

Carl Barth (1860-1939)

Innovation: Mathematical Formulas

Developed mathematical methods for machine speeds, tool angles, and work standards.

BHEL Application: Uses mathematical precision for power plant equipment - optimal machine settings, tool life calculations, quality standards.

Harrington Emerson (1853-1931)

Innovation: 12 Principles of Efficiency

Focused on organization-wide efficiency, not just individual workers.

Modern Consulting: McKinsey, BCG use Emerson's efficiency principles to help Indian companies improve performance.

🎯 Key Takeaways - Followers of Taylor:

  • Each follower specialized in different aspects of management
  • Gantt: Planning and scheduling, Gilbreths: Motion study, Barth: Mathematical precision
  • Their principles still used in modern project management and operations
  • Indian companies successfully adapted these techniques to local conditions

5. Problems with Scientific Management

Simple Definition:

While Scientific Management improved efficiency, it faced criticism from three groups: employers, employees, and psychologists. Like a medicine that cures one disease but causes side effects.

Who Criticized Main Problems Why They Were Unhappy Indian Example
👔 Employers • High setup costs
• Need skilled managers
• Resistance to change
• May not work everywhere		 Expensive to implement, requires investment in training and systems Maruti Suzuki's ₹500+ crore investment in Japanese methods
👷 Workers • Felt like robots
• Job security fears
• Lost creativity
• Exploitation worries		 Boring repetitive work, fear of working themselves out of jobs 1920s Mumbai textile mill strikes, Kashmir artisans' resistance
🧠 Psychologists • Ignored social needs
• Treated all workers same
• Neglected emotions
• Created stress		 Humans are social beings with different motivations, not machines IT industry burnout, call center stress in Indian BPOs

🎭 The Human Side Story:

A textile worker in 1920s Mumbai said: "I used to make complete shirts and felt proud. Now I only attach buttons all day. I feel like a machine, not a human."

Modern Solutions:

  • Google's Approach: 20% time for creative projects alongside systematic work
  • Tata's Balance: Efficiency with employee welfare and ethical practices
  • Infosys Innovation: Scientific methods + employee suggestion systems

🎯 Key Takeaways - Problems with Scientific Management:

  • Improved efficiency but ignored human factors
  • Led to development of Human Relations Movement
  • Modern management balances efficiency with employee satisfaction
  • Successful companies integrate scientific methods with human-centered approaches

Administrative Theory

Henri Fayol (1841-1925) - Father of Modern Management

6. Fayol's Management Activities

Simple Definition:

Fayol's Management Activities are like the different roles a cricket captain plays - sometimes planning strategy, organizing team, leading players, coordinating efforts, and checking if the plan is working.

🎭 School Play Director Analogy:

A play director plans the play, organizes actors and props, commands during rehearsals, coordinates everyone's efforts, and controls the final performance.

Memory Trick: "POCCC" = Plan, Organize, Command, Coordinate, Control

📋 1. Planning

What it means: Decide what you want to achieve and how you'll get there.
Reliance Jio: 3 years planning 4G launch - tower locations, pricing, marketing. Result: 100 million customers in record time!

🏗️ 2. Organizing

What it means: Arrange people and resources so work flows smoothly.
ISRO Mars Mission: Teams organized across cities - spacecraft (Bangalore), launch (Sriharikota), tracking (Pune).

👨‍💼 3. Command (Leading)

What it means: Guide and motivate people to do their best work.
Ratan Tata: During 26/11 crisis, provided clear direction and motivated employees under pressure.

🔗 4. Coordination

What it means: Make sure different teams work together, not against each other.
Indian Railways: Coordinates 12,000+ trains daily across operations, maintenance, commercial, security teams.

✅ 5. Control

What it means: Monitor progress and fix problems when things go off track.
Domino's: Real-time pizza tracking, automatic alerts if delays, immediate corrective action.

🎯 Key Takeaways - Fayol's Management Activities:

  • All five activities are interconnected - you can't skip any one
  • Every manager performs all activities, regardless of level or industry
  • Planning comes first, control comes last, but all happen continuously
  • Universal framework applicable across all organizations

7. Functions of Manager

Simple Definition:

Functions of Manager are the essential roles every manager must perform, like a Swiss Army knife - multiple tools needed to be effective.

📱 Ola Cabs Manager Example:

An Ola city manager performs all these functions daily:

📊 Planning Function

  • Forecasting: Predict demand patterns
  • Setting objectives: Revenue targets
  • Strategy: Competitive pricing
  • Budgeting: Marketing spend

🏗️ Organizing Function

  • Structure: Team hierarchy
  • Job design: Driver onboarding
  • Resources: Office, technology
  • Authority: Decision-making power

👥 Staffing Function

  • Recruitment: Hire drivers, support staff
  • Training: App usage, customer service
  • Performance: Rating systems
  • Compensation: Fair payment structure

🎯 Directing Function

  • Leadership: Motivate team during challenges
  • Communication: Clear instructions
  • Decision making: Resolve conflicts
  • Motivation: Incentive programs

✅ Controlling Function

  • Standards: Response time targets
  • Monitoring: Real-time dashboards
  • Comparison: Actual vs targets
  • Correction: Action plans for gaps

🏆 Success Example - Swiggy Growth:

Planning: 500 cities by 2020
Organizing: City-wise teams
Staffing: 200,000+ delivery partners
Directing: Customer obsession culture
Controlling: 30-minute delivery tracking

Integration: All functions work together. Weakness in one affects others. Modern technology enhances but doesn't replace these basic functions.

🎯 Key Takeaways - Functions of Manager:

  • Every manager performs all five functions, but emphasis varies by level
  • Functions remain constant, but methods evolve with technology
  • Success requires competence in all functions, not just one or two
  • Functions are interconnected and continuous, not sequential

8. Henry Fayol's 14 Principles of Management

Simple Definition:

Fayol's 14 Principles are like traffic rules for management - universal guidelines that help organizations run smoothly, regardless of size, type, or location.

Memory Trick: "DASU REUS SICS" - Remember as two groups of principles

1. 🔧 Division of Work

Idea: Let people specialize in what they're good at.

Indian Railways: Station Masters (operations), Commercial Clerks (ticketing), Technical Staff (maintenance), RPF (security).

2. ⚖️ Authority & Responsibility

Idea: Power must equal accountability.

ISRO Mission Director: Authority to spend ₹450 crores, responsible for mission success.

3. 📏 Discipline

Idea: Respect rules and agreements.

Tata Group: Code of Conduct applies to everyone from CEO to entry-level.

4. 👨‍💼 Unity of Command

Idea: One boss rule - avoid confusion.

Indian Army: Clear chain of command ensures quick decisions, no confusion.

5. 🎯 Unity of Direction

Idea: One plan for same objective.

Reliance Jio: All departments focused on single 4G launch strategy.

6. 🤝 Subordination of Interest

Idea: Organization goals come first.

Tata 26/11: Employees put guest safety above personal safety.

7. 💰 Remuneration

Idea: Fair pay for work done.

Zerodha: Above-market salaries + profit sharing for all employees.

8. 🏢 Centralization

Idea: Right balance of central control.

SBI: Central policies, local operational flexibility.

9. 🪜 Scalar Chain

Idea: Clear line of authority top to bottom.

Railways Hierarchy: Minister → Secretary → Board → GM → DRM → Station Master.

10. 📊 Order

Idea: Everything has its proper place.

Dabbawalas: Color coding, specific routes, systematic organization.

11. ⚖️ Equity

Idea: Fair treatment for everyone.

Godrej Group: Inclusive policies for LGBTQ+, women, differently-abled.

12. 🏠 Stability of Tenure

Idea: Job security builds loyalty.

TCS: No-layoff policy results in lower attrition than industry average.

13. 💡 Initiative

Idea: Encourage creativity and ideas.

3M India: 15% time policy leads to innovations and patents.

14. 🤝 Esprit de Corps

Idea: Build team spirit and unity.

Indian Cricket: MS Dhoni's leadership created unified team, won World Cups.

🎯 Key Takeaways - Fayol's 14 Principles:

  • Universal principles applicable across all industries and cultures
  • Provide framework for organizing and managing people effectively
  • Balance structural efficiency with human factors
  • Modern organizations adapt these principles to contemporary contexts

Systems Approach

Modern Holistic Management Perspective

9. Systems Approach to Management

EVERYTHING IS CONNECTED

🔄 INPUTS → TRANSFORMATION → OUTPUTS
↑_______ FEEDBACK _______↓

Like the human body - all parts work together

Simple Definition:

Systems Approach views organization as a complex system of interconnected parts working together, like the human body where different organs work together to keep the body healthy.

🌐 WhatsApp Message Journey:

When you send a WhatsApp message, it travels through your phone → cell tower → internet → servers → recipient's network → their phone. All systems working together seamlessly!

🛒 Flipkart System Example:

Inputs: Products, technology, delivery partners, customer data
Transformation: Order processing, warehousing, logistics
Outputs: Delivered products, satisfied customers
Feedback: Reviews, ratings, repeat purchases

🔍 Systems Theory

Key Idea: Organizations are complex systems that interact with their environment.

🌐 Open System

Characteristics: Interacts with environment, adapts to changes, exchanges inputs/outputs.

🔒 Closed System

Characteristics: Self-contained, no environmental interaction (rare in reality).

⭐ System Properties

  • Holism: Whole > sum of parts
  • Synergy: 1+1 can equal 3
  • Interdependence: Everything affects everything
  • Equifinality: Multiple paths to same goal

🏏 IPL System Example (Synergy):

Individual teams might struggle financially, but IPL system creates enormous value - ₹11,000+ crores annually, much more than sum of individual team revenues.

Components of Organizational System:

  • Inputs: Human resources, materials, money, information
  • Transformation: Processes that convert inputs to outputs
  • Outputs: Products, services, satisfied customers
  • Feedback: Information about results that influences future inputs
  • Environment: External factors affecting the system
Advantages of Systems Approach: Better coordination, focus on objectives, environmental awareness, enhanced innovation, improved problem-solving.

🎯 Key Takeaways - Systems Approach:

  • Organizations are open systems constantly interacting with environment
  • Understanding connections and relationships is crucial for success
  • Synergy means combined effect exceeds individual effects
  • Modern digital businesses are perfect examples of systems thinking
  • Systems approach helps manage complexity in modern organizations
