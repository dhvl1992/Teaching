The Taylor Revolution

How One Engineer Changed the World of Work

Taylor's Big Idea: Work Smarter, Not Harder

ü§î The Problem Taylor Saw:

In the 1880s, workers basically guessed how to do their jobs. No standards, no training, no systematic approach. It was like everyone cooking without recipes - some good results, mostly chaos!

Taylor's Solution: The "One Best Way"

Taylor believed there was a scientifically best way to do every job. Just like there's a best way to solve a math problem, there should be a best way to make a product or deliver a service.

Memory Trick: "SSTMDM" = Smart Students Think More, Do More

1. üî¨ Scientific Task Study

Concept: Break big jobs into small pieces, study each piece, find the best way.

ISRO Example: Mars mission wasn't just "go to Mars." It was broken into thousands of specific tasks - fuel calculations, navigation programming, communication protocols. Each task was studied scientifically. Result: Mission succeeded for just ‚Çπ450 crores!

2. üë• Right Person, Right Job

Concept: Choose people based on their abilities, not politics or favoritism.

Indian Railways: To become a train driver, you need physical fitness tests, psychological assessments, and technical exams. They don't just hire anyone - they scientifically match person to job.

3. üéì Proper Training

Concept: Teach people the right way systematically, don't just hope they figure it out.

Infosys: New engineers don't jump straight into projects. They spend 3 months in systematic training at Mysore campus, learning standardized methods.

4. ‚è±Ô∏è Time & Motion Study

Concept: Study how people move and work, eliminate waste, optimize timing.

Mumbai Dabbawalas: Every movement is timed and optimized - from collecting tiffins to train transfers. This precision gives them 99.999% accuracy.

5. üí∞ Pay for Performance

Concept: Reward people based on how well they perform, not just showing up.

Flipkart: During Big Billion Days, warehouse workers get bonuses for meeting targets, quality bonuses for zero errors, and team bonuses for overall performance.

6. ü§ù Mental Revolution

Concept: Change from "us vs them" to "let's work together to make things better for everyone."

Tata Group: During 26/11 Mumbai attacks, Tata employees and management worked together to protect guests, putting collective good above individual safety.

Time & Motion Study: The Art of Eliminating Waste

TWO SIMPLE QUESTIONS:

‚è∞ How long should this take?
üéØ What movements are necessary?

Eliminate waste, optimize flow

The Basic Idea:

Time Study: Find the standard time needed for a task (like timing how long it takes to solve different types of math problems).

Motion Study: Eliminate unnecessary movements (like organizing your desk so everything you need is within reach).

Fayol's Management Framework

The Art of Managing People and Organizations

Fayol's Simple Truth: What All Managers Do

üé≠ Think of a School Play Director:

A play director plans the play, organizes actors and props, commands during rehearsals, coordinates everyone's efforts, and controls the final performance. That's exactly what Fayol said all managers do!

Memory Trick: "POCCC" = Plan, Organize, Command, Coordinate, Control

üìã Planning: Think Ahead

What it means: Decide what you want to achieve and how you'll get there.
Reliance Jio Example: Spent 3 years planning their 4G launch - where to put towers, how to price services, when to launch. Result: 100 million customers in record time!

üèóÔ∏è Organizing: Get Set Up

What it means: Arrange people and resources so work can happen smoothly.
ISRO Example: For Mars mission, they organized teams across different cities - spacecraft team in Bangalore, launch team in Sriharikota, tracking team in Pune.

üë®‚Äçüíº Command: Lead the Way

What it means: Guide and motivate people to do their best work.
Ratan Tata Example: During 26/11 crisis, he provided clear direction, motivated employees, and made tough decisions under pressure.

üîó Coordinate: Work Together

What it means: Make sure different teams work in harmony, not against each other.
Indian Railways: Coordinates 12,000+ trains daily - operations, maintenance, commercial, and security teams all working together.

‚úÖ Control: Check and Correct

What it means: Monitor progress and fix problems when things go off track.
Domino's Example: Real-time tracking of pizza delivery, automatic alerts if delays occur, immediate corrective action.
Universal Truth: Whether you're managing a cricket team, planning a college fest, or running a startup - you'll use all five activities!

Fayol's 14 Universal Rules (Made Simple)

üß† Memory Trick: "DASU REUS SICS"

Division, Authority, Scalar, Unity Command, Remuneration, Equity, Unity Direction, Stability, Subordination, Initiative, Centralization, Sprit de Corps

üîß Division of Work

Simple idea: Let people specialize in what they're good at.

Example: In restaurants, someone cooks, someone serves, someone handles cash. Everyone gets really good at their part.

‚öñÔ∏è Authority = Responsibility

Simple idea: If you give someone power, they must be accountable for results.

Example: ISRO mission director had authority to spend ‚Çπ450 crores but was responsible for mission success.

üë®‚Äçüíº One Boss Rule

Simple idea: Each person should get orders from only one boss to avoid confusion.

Example: In Indian Army, every soldier knows exactly who gives them orders - no confusion, quick decisions.

üí∞ Fair Pay

Simple idea: Pay people fairly based on their contribution.

Example: Zerodha pays above-market salaries and shares profits with all employees, leading to high satisfaction and low turnover.

üìä Order & Organization

Simple idea: Everything should have its proper place.

Example: Mumbai Dabbawalas use color coding and specific routes - systematic organization leads to amazing efficiency.

‚öñÔ∏è Treat People Fairly

Simple idea: Be fair and just with everyone, but fair doesn't always mean identical.

Example: Tata Group's inclusive policies - different support for different needs, but fair treatment for all.

üè† Job Security

Simple idea: Give people stability so they can develop skills and loyalty.

Example: TCS's no-layoff policy creates loyal, skilled workforce with lower turnover than competitors.

üí° Encourage Ideas

Simple idea: Let people use their creativity and initiative.

Example: 3M India's "15% time" policy allows employees to work on personal projects, leading to innovations and patents.

ü§ù Team Spirit

Simple idea: Build unity and team spirit - "together we're stronger."

Example: Indian cricket team's transformation under MS Dhoni - from individual stars to unified team, leading to World Cup victories.

Systems Thinking: Seeing the Big Picture

EVERYTHING IS CONNECTED

üîÑ INPUT ‚Üí PROCESS ‚Üí OUTPUT
‚Üë_______ FEEDBACK _______‚Üì

Like the human body - all parts work together

üåê The WhatsApp Message Journey:

When you send a WhatsApp message, it travels through dozens of systems - your phone, cell tower, internet backbone, servers, recipient's network, their phone. All working together seamlessly. That's systems thinking!

Key Systems Ideas:

  • Holism: The whole is greater than sum of parts (like a cricket team vs individual players)
  • Interdependence: Everything affects everything else
  • Synergy: 1+1 can equal 3 when things work together well
  • Feedback: Results influence future inputs

Flipkart Example:

Inputs: Products, technology, delivery partners
Process: Order management, warehousing, logistics
Outputs: Delivered products, customer satisfaction
Feedback: Reviews, ratings, repeat purchases

Putting It All Together: Three Different Lenses

üéØ Think of Building a Cricket Team:

Taylor's approach: Focus on each player's technique - perfect batting stance, bowling action, fielding positions.

Fayol's approach: Focus on team management - captain's leadership, coordination between players, game strategy.

Systems approach: Focus on everything - players, management, fans, weather, pitch conditions, and how they all interact.
