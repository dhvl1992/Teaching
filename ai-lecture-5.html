Understanding the Limitations

üöß Three Major Problems

üèîÔ∏è Local Maxima: Peaks that aren't the highest

Like reaching the top of a small hill when Mount Everest is nearby but hidden by fog.

üèîÔ∏è Plateaus: Flat areas with no uphill direction

Like walking on a mesa - everywhere you look is the same height!

üèîÔ∏è Ridges: Narrow peaks with steep sides

Progress requires going diagonally, but you can only move north/south/east/west.

For 8-Queens: Hill climbing succeeds only 14% of the time, but takes just 4 steps when it works!

üö∂ Hill Climbing with Random Walk

The Problem

When landscape has local minima, any search that moves only in greedy direction cannot be complete.

Random walk, on the other hand, is asymptotically complete

The Solution

Put random walk into greedy hill-climbing

At each step do one of two:

  • Greedy: With prob p move to neighbor with largest value
  • Random: With prob 1-p move to random neighbor
Balances exploitation (greedy) with exploration (random)

üéØ Stochastic Hill-Climbing Variations

Three Powerful Variations:

1. Stochastic Hill-Climbing
Random selection among uphill moves, with probability varying by steepness
2. Random-Walk Hill-Climbing
Mix greedy moves with random exploration
3. Hill-Climbing with Both
At each step choose from: Greedy, Random Walk, or Random Restart
8-Queens with Sideways Moves:
‚Ä¢ Allow up to 100 sideways moves when stuck
‚Ä¢ Success rate increases from 14% to 94%
‚Ä¢ But: 21 steps for success, 64 steps for failure

Key Insight: Sometimes you need to go sideways or even downhill temporarily to find better paths!

üî• Simulated Annealing

üå°Ô∏è Cooling Metal to Perfect Crystal

Physics Meets Search

Inspired by metallurgy: heating metal makes atoms move randomly, slowly cooling allows perfect crystal formation.

  • High Temperature: Accept bad moves frequently
  • Gradual Cooling: Become more selective over time
  • Low Temperature: Behave like hill climbing
  • Result: Escape local maxima early, converge later
Key Formula: Accept worse move with probability e^(ŒîE/T)
Where ŒîE is change in quality, T is temperature

üéØ Key Takeaways

Local search trades completeness for efficiency and memory savings
  • Hill Climbing: Simple but gets stuck in local optima
  • Random Restarts: Usually the best practical choice
  • Random Walk: Adds exploration to escape traps
  • Stochastic Variations: Balance greedy and random moves
  • Simulated Annealing: Elegant physics-inspired approach
  • Genetic Algorithms: Population-based evolution
When to use: When you need a good solution quickly and the path doesn't matter - just the final state!

These algorithms power everything from AI training to industrial design optimization. You've learned the foundations of modern optimization!
